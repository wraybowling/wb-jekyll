The 48 Hour Film Project is an annual film-making competition. Grey Justice made its debut for Richmond's 2010's competition, and took home two awards. Grey Justice was Richmond's only animated entry.

48 Hour Film Project Richmond: Winner of "Best Use of Character" & "Best Special Effects"

Wray's particular part played in Grey Justice, though small, was a fantastic opportunity to work with some of the Blender community's finest 3D animators. The project was ambitiously headed by Jason Van Gumster, publisher of Blender For Dummies and the lead of Hand Turkey Studios. The "International team" worked through Redmine, SVN, and Blender's linking system to complete shots among multiple animators across multiple countries over the course of three epic sleepless nights.
