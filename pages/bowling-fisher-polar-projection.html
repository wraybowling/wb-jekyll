Bill Fisher and I are fond of maps. This map, which we affectionately called the Bowling-Fisher Polar Projection is a purely custom-made map of Earth printed on fabric. It features no slices on any land masses except Greenland, a reduced amount of stretching when compared to the Mercatur Projection (which we loathe), and a north pole center.

Greenland has been abused on maps for centuries it seems. Like respectable cartographers, we messed up Greenland on purpose.

All maps of the Earth are projections. In order to project our map, I used Blender's tools which are meant for texture mapping and texture map baking. These tools are typically used to reduce the amount of stretching on the projection and make it easy to project from one arbitrary surface to another.

The setup involved using two spheres and an image taken from NASA's Blue Marble project. For no particular reason, I chose the sea-level image because it could be easily clamped to produce a distinction between land versus water.

The entire 5400x5400 image was then piped through Adobe Illustrator's Live Trace to produce a crisp beautiful vector image.
